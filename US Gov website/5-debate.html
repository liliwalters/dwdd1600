The Floor Debate

The full chamber debates the bill:

  • House: Debate is strictly timed and controlled by the rules set earlier.
  • Senate: Debate is more open-ended. Senators can speak for as long as they wish, this is called a filibuster, where a senator or group talks indefinitely to block a vote. Ending a filibuster requires a cloture vote of 60 senators

During floor debate, members can propose floor amendments to modify the bill further
