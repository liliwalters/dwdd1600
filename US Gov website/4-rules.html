The Rules Committee (House Only)

In the House, before a bill reaches the full floor, it must pass through the Rules Committee, which sets the terms for debate, for example, how long it will last, what amendments can be offered, and under what conditions. This is sometimes called the "traffic cop" of the House. The Senate does not have this step, instead, debate terms are typically negotiated by Senate leaders.
